NOCTUA

Autonomous Drone for Anti-Poaching

Poaching remains a critical threat to wildlife, with approximately 96 elephants and two rhinos killed daily in Africa. Traditional surveillance methods are often inadequate, especially in vast and remote game reserves.

In response, NOCTUA began as an ETH Zürich Focus Project, where a team of ten Bachelor students collaborated to develop a silent, long-endurance drone capable of autonomously monitoring large areas for up to 12 hours. This initiative aimed to provide rangers with an effective tool to detect poachers and monitor wildlife without disturbing the natural habitat.

Building on the initial success, NOCTUA has transitioned into a dedicated research project under ETH Zürich's Soft Robotics Lab. The project now focuses on enhancing the drone's capabilities, including integrating advanced sensors and AI-driven navigation systems to improve performance in low-visibility conditions.

As a member of the software subteam, my responsibilities encompass:

  • Designing the geolocation module to accurately map detected heat signatures.
  • Integrating thermal imaging recognition to identify and track wildlife and potential threats in real-time.
  • Tuning the flight controller parameters to ensure stable and efficient flight performance.
  • Conducting field tests to validate system functionality and gather data for further improvements.

Through these efforts, NOCTUA aims to provide a robust and reliable solution for wildlife conservation, leveraging cutting-edge technology to support rangers in their mission to protect endangered species.
