NOCTUA

Autonomous Drone for Anti-Poaching

Rhino poaching in South Africa rose by 9,000% in the last 15 years, posing a significant challenge to wildlife reserves in monitoring extensive game parks. The main struggle stems from the lack of efficient surveillance solutions, as commercial multi-rotor drones offer limited flight times of about 15 to 55 minutes and cause noise pollution.

In response to this challenge, what began as an ETH Zürich Focus-Project with 10 Bachelor students has evolved into a dedicated research project, pushing the boundaries of technology. We're developing a drone capable of flying an entire night, equipping rangers with an Eye in the Sky that is silent and unnoticed by animals and poachers.

My direct responsibilities include designing and implementing the software architecture and developing the integrated drone control systems.
