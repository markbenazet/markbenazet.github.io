NOCTUA
ETH Zürich · Soft Robotics Lab

NOCTUA — Long Endurance Autonomous UAV

NOCTUA is an ongoing research project at ETH Zürich dedicated to developing a silent, long-endurance fixed-wing UAV designed to support and monitor wildlife. The platform enables autonomous aerial monitoring for up to 12 hours, assisting operations such as anti-poaching efforts, wildfire detection, and wildlife tracking.

As a member of the software subteam, I contribute to the integration of onboard sensors with the Pixhawk flight controller, the development of the geolocation pipeline, and the implementation of real-time thermal imaging for heat-based object detection. My work also involves tuning control parameters and conducting field tests to validate autonomous navigation and system performance.

  • Integration of sensors and flight controller (Pixhawk)
  • Development of ROS 2-based geolocation and mapping modules
  • Real-time thermal imaging and heat-signature detection
  • Control tuning and field validation of autonomous missions

Current Project
Semester Thesis
UZH · Robotics and Perception Group

Learning Agile Drone Maneuvers from VLM-Generated Preferences

Developing a preference-based reinforcement learning framework for agile quadrotor flight. The system leverages vision-language models to autonomously generate preference labels, removing the need for handcrafted reward functions or manual evaluations. Implementation and real-world testing are conducted on physical drones to validate performance and robustness.

  • Preference-based Reinforcement Learning
  • Vision-Language Model integration
  • Agile quadrotor control and flight testing

Past Project
PDM4AR Spaceship Navigation
ETH Zürich · Institute for Dynamic Systems and Control

Sequential Convexification for Autonomous Spaceship Navigation and Docking

Developed a complete planning stack for autonomous spaceship navigation in complex space environments with moving obstacles. Implemented Sequential Convexification (SCvx) methodology, the same approach used by SpaceX for autonomous landing, to generate fuel-optimal trajectories while satisfying nonlinear dynamics, collision avoidance, and precise docking constraints.

  • Sequential Convexification (SCvx) for trajectory optimization
  • Nonlinear dynamics modeling with Zero/First-Order Hold discretization
  • Multi-scenario planning: static obstacles, moving satellites, and precision docking
  • Convex optimization with CVXPY for real-time trajectory generation
Past Project
PDM4AR Autonomous Highway Driving
ETH Zürich · Institute for Dynamic Systems and Control

Motion Planning and Control for Autonomous Highway Lane Changing

Developed a complete planning and control stack for autonomous highway driving in mixed traffic environments. Implemented risk-aware trajectory planning using kinematic bicycle model dynamics to perform safe and efficient lane-changing maneuvers among human-driven vehicles, validated on real-world traffic scenarios from the CommonRoad dataset.

  • Risk-aware motion planning with time-to-collision metrics
  • Kinematic bicycle model for vehicle dynamics
  • Sensor occlusion handling with 360° Lidar observations
  • Real-time trajectory optimization for dense traffic scenarios
